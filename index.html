What are reports?
Reports are files you can download from SumUp to better understand your business activity. They include information such as sales, payouts, fees, taxes, and transactions, and can help you track performance, reconcile payments, or share data with your accountant.

How reports connect to each other
Reports are different views of the same business activity. One report might show what you sold, another shows how customers paid, another explains what was paid out to your bank account, and another gives you the documents you need for accounting or tax.
For example, if a customer pays €100 by card:
📈
Sales report
Shows the €100 sale
›
🔄
Transactions report
Shows the individual card payment
›
🏦
Payout report
Shows when it reached your bank account
›
🧮
Fees report
Shows any SumUp fees charged
›
📋
Tax / fiscal report
Shows the tax breakdown for accounting
⚠️ Why numbers might look different across reports

These reports may not always show the same total — they answer different questions. A sales report focuses on when the sale happened. A payout report focuses on when money was sent to your bank. A tax report focuses on what's needed for compliance.

If numbers look different, check:
  • Are you looking at the same date range?
  • Are you comparing sales date with payout date?
  • Were any fees deducted?
  • Were there refunds, chargebacks, or adjustments?
  • Does the report include tips, discounts, cash payments, or taxes?
  • Is the report based on a daily closing period, calendar day, or month?
The easiest way to think about it:
Sales reportsexplain what happened in your business
Item reportsexplain what you sold
Transaction reportsexplain individual payments
Payout reportsexplain what reached your bank account
Invoices & tax reportssupport accounting, tax, and compliance

Together, they give you the full picture — from the moment a customer pays to the moment the money is recorded, paid out, and reported for accounting.
