Reports Guide

Everything you need to know about your business data

What are reports?
Reports are files you can download from SumUp to better understand your business activity. They include information such as sales, payouts, fees, taxes, and transactions, and can help you track performance, reconcile payments, or share data with your accountant.
What will reports help you do?

Understand your sales

See your total revenue and how it breaks down by payment method, refunds, discounts, tips, and more.

Track what you sold

Review item-level sales, including quantities, variations, and revenue per product where available.

Follow your money

Match payments to the payouts sent to your bank account, including fees, refunds, and deductions.

Handle taxes & accounting

Download reports commonly needed for bookkeeping, tax filing, invoices, and fiscal exports.

Close out your day

Get a daily POS or Kiosk summary with totals, Z-number, and payment method breakdown.

How reports connect to each other
Reports are different views of the same business activity. One report might show what you sold, another shows how customers paid, another explains what was paid out to your bank account, and another gives you the documents you need for accounting or tax.

Frequently asked questions
Quick answers to the questions we hear most.
Log in to your SumUp dashboard, then look for Reports in the left-hand navigation menu. From there, you'll see all available reports listed. Select the one you need, choose your date range, and hit Download.
Yes — most reports let you set a custom date range before downloading. Simply select your start and end dates in the date picker and then download. The Fees report is generated monthly and covers a fixed calendar month.
Payments are individual transactions — each time a customer pays you. Payouts are the batch transfers SumUp sends to your bank account, which bundle multiple payments together. Think of it like this: Payments are the ingredients, Payouts are the delivery.
For most accountants, the Sales report (detailed breakdown per transaction) and the Fees report (your SumUp costs) are the most useful. If you issue invoices through SumUp, also share the Invoices report.
Some reports are only generated when there is relevant activity. For example, the End of Day report only appears if you used a SumUp POS or Kiosk that day. The Fees report is generated monthly — you won't see it until the end of the billing month. Also check that your selected date range covers the period you're looking for.
Most reports are available across all SumUp markets. However, some reports — like POS Fiscal Exports — are specific to certain countries where local tax authorities require a particular data format. If you don't see a report listed, it may not apply to your country.
Currently, reports are downloaded manually from the dashboard. They are generated in real time when you request them, so the data is always up to date. You can download as often as you need.
